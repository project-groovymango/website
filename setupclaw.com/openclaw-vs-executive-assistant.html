What each does well

OpenClaw excels at:

  • 24/7 availability — Works while you sleep. Processes overnight emails and has everything triaged by morning.
  • Speed — Responds to scheduling requests in seconds, not hours.
  • Consistency — Never has a bad day, never forgets a follow-up, never loses context.
  • Scale — Handles 200+ emails/day without slowing down. Deploy multiple agents for different team members at $1,500 each.
  • No management overhead — No 1-on-1s, no PTO, no performance reviews, no turnover.

Human EAs excel at:

  • Judgment calls — Knowing when to escalate, reading social dynamics, handling sensitive situations with nuance.
  • Physical tasks — Booking travel, making reservations, handling deliveries, managing office logistics.
  • Relationship building — Remembering personal details, sending handwritten notes, managing gifts.
  • Complex multi-step tasks — Planning events, coordinating board meetings, managing vendor relationships.
  • Emotional intelligence — Knowing when a task is urgent vs. when the boss is just stressed.

When to choose OpenClaw

  • You need help now, not in 3 months after hiring and training.
  • Your primary bottleneck is email, scheduling, and communication — not physical tasks.
  • You want an assistant for multiple team members without multiplying headcount.
  • You're a 4–50 person company where a full-time EA isn't justifiable yet.
  • You need 24/7 coverage across time zones.

When to hire a human EA instead

  • You need travel management, event planning, or physical logistics.
  • Your role involves high-stakes, nuanced communication that requires human judgment on every message.
  • You prefer a human relationship with your assistant.
  • You're at a scale where a full-time EA is a rounding error in your operating budget.

Why not both?

Several SetupClaw customers use OpenClaw alongside a human EA. The AI handles the high-volume, routine work (inbox triage, scheduling, daily briefings) while the human EA focuses on high-judgment tasks (relationship management, event planning, complex coordination). This combination gives the EA leverage and lets them focus on the work that actually requires a human.
